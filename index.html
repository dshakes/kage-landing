how it works

Three steps, one evening.

01

Plug it in.

The Kage box lives on your home network. One cable, one install script, one YubiKey. No account, no cloud.

02

Drop your data.

Drag emails, notes, folders into the console. Kage encrypts everything on your laptop before a byte leaves. It auto-routes each file into search, voice training, or facts.

03

Ask, act, automate.

Chat from the browser, CLI, or WhatsApp. Kage answers from your files, drafts emails in your voice, and confirms with you before it acts on anything real.

questions

What people ask us.

Do I need to be technical to run this?

No. One installer, a 5-step wizard, and a non-technical settings page. Power users get a CLI and full cluster access.

What hardware do I need?

Minimum: one NVIDIA GPU with 24GB VRAM, 32GB RAM, 500GB SSD. Recommended: a 48GB GPU for the full 72B model. We sell a turn-key box if you don't want to pick parts.

Can my family each have their own space?

Yes. Primary, partner, child roles; every member gets private, household, and (for children) parent-visible spaces. Different keys, different guardrails, all one appliance.

Can Kage talk to me on WhatsApp / Slack / iMessage / voice?

Yes. A built-in gateway bridges each channel to the same assistant — still fully private, still audited.

What if my hardware breaks?

Your data is age-encrypted to recipients only you hold. Restore on any new box by pointing it at the backup and tapping your YubiKey.

Do you train on my data?

We have no servers. We never see your data. Fine-tuning happens on your own hardware, on your own LoRA adapters.

Does it work fully offline?

Yes. The lite profile runs end-to-end on a single box with zero egress: compact Qwen 2.5 on llama.cpp, SQLite for storage, age-file secrets. Pull the ethernet cable; Kage still answers.

How do I plug in Gmail, WhatsApp, iMessage, Slack?

Each has a Connect card in the console. Gmail and Slack are OAuth. WhatsApp supports personal (QR-scan from your phone, via a local bridge) and Business (Meta Cloud API). iMessage pairs with a BlueBubbles bridge running on a Mac you own — private network only.
